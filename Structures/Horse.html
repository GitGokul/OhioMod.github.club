FIGURE S.4. Characterizing the stability and Daunorubicin of Horse. A. Isometric view of Horse measuring 92.5nm x 15.0 nm. B. Horse is a 5x6 square lattice. C. Typical zoomed out TEM image of Horse. D. Image average of 21 images of Horse. E. MgCl2 stability gel where lane L is 1kb ladder, FS is PEG-purified structure. Structures were PEG-purified and resuspended in varying concentrations of MgCl2 for 24hrs. Structures were then loaded in a 2% agarose gel. As shown Horse degrades at 1mM MgCl2. F. TEM images of Horse at 3, 1, 0mM MgCl2. G.Normalized gel intensity of Horse at varying MgCl2 concentrations. H. Fetal Bovine Serum (FBS) stability gel at room temperature (25OC). Structures were PEG-purified and resuspended in varying concentrations of FBS for 24hrs. Structures were then PEG-purified to remove nucleases and then loaded in a 2% agarose gel. I. TEM images of Horse at 50, 75, 100% FBS. J. Normalized gel intensity of Horse at varying FBS concentrations. K. Fetal Bovine Serum (FBS) stability gel at physiological temperature (37OC). Structures were PEG-purified and resuspended in varying concentrations of FBS for 24hrs. Structures were then PEG-purified to remove nucleases and then loaded in a 2% agarose gel. L. TEM images of Horse at 50, 75, 100% FBS. M. Normalized gel intensity of Horse at varying FBS concentrations. N. Average Base Pair Binding Ratio (BBR) of Horse for 10, 15, 20nM and varying concentrations of origami structure after 24hrs of incubation. 10nM of Horse has the highest BBR. O. TEM images of 10nM, 15nM, and 20nM Horse after 24hr incubation. Note the twisted geometry of daunorubicin-incorporated structure versus daunorubicin-free structure. Scale Bars 100nm unless otherwise indicated.
