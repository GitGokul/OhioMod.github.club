Daunorubicin Steps:

  1. Add 100 uL of structure and 100 uL of 15% PEG (in 1xFOB with 500 mM NaCl) to a 1.5 mL epi tube
  2. Centrifuge the epi tube for 25 minutes at 16000 G
  3. Carefully remove the supernatant
  4. Resuspend the structure by adding 50 uL of 1xFOB with 20 mM MgCl2. Pipet up and down to mix
  5. Dilute the structure to 20 nM by adding the desired volume of 1xFOB with 20 mM MgCl2
  6. Repeat steps 1-3
  7. Resuspend the structure by adding 50 uL of 1xPBS with 10 mM MgCl2 to the epi tube. Pipet up and down to mix
  8. Dilute the sample to 20 nM by adding the desired amount of buffer solution
  9. Set aside 15 uL of sample to be used for TEM imaging later
  10. To get to a desired volume of 100 uL, add 2.5 uL of 10 mM daunorubicin, and the calculated amounts of structure and 1xPBS with 10 mM MgCl2
  11. Add daunorubicin, structure, and 1xPBS with 10 mM MgCl2. Pipet up and down to mix
  12. Incubate the sample for 24 hours at 37 degrees C
  13. Centrifuge the epi tube for 25 minutes at 16000 G
  14. Carefully remove the supernatant. This will be used to test the amount of daunorubicin that the structure holds
  15. Resuspend the structure by adding 100 uL of 1xPBS with 10 mM MgCl2 to the epi tube. Pipet up and down to mix
  16. Save the sample for TEM imaging
  17. Using a nanodrop spectrophotometer, measure daunorubicin concentration at a wavelength of 480 nm
  18. Test 1 uL of saved supernatant for its concentration, using the standard curve for daunorubicin. Repeat twice and average the results
  19. Calculate the base pair binding ratio of daunorubicin to structure using the Base Pair Binding Ratio MATLAB code (See suplemental info)
